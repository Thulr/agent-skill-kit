# General Prose Playbook

## Scope

- **In:** short-form nonfiction with no specialized structural template, where
  clarity and flow are the whole job — emails, announcements, blog posts, update
  notes, descriptions, short explanations.
- **Out:** a structured argument (→ `argument-memo.md`), a step-by-step
  procedure (→ `technical-doc.md`), a pitch or talk (→ `talk-pitch.md`), a
  long-form story (→ `narrative.md`).
- **Intents this surface answers:** `structure`, `draft` (design); `revise`,
  `copyedit`, `diagnose` (audit).

## Grounding

- *On Writing Well* (William Zinsser, 1976) — strip clutter; plain words over
  inflated ones; rewriting is where clarity is made.
- *The Sense of Style* (Steven Pinker, 2014) — direct, concrete style; the
  expert blind spot; given-before-new ordering.
- *Dreyer's English* (Benjamin Dreyer, 2019) — ban the wan intensifiers and
  throat-clearers; mechanics in service of the writer's intent.
- *Bird by Bird* (Anne Lamott, 1994) — permission to write a rough first pass;
  shrink the task.
- *Revising Prose* (Richard Lanham, 1979) — the tightening pass and a
  before/after word ratio.

## Good signals

- One clear point; the reader knows it by the end of the first short paragraph.
- Plain, concrete words; no inflated diction or undefined jargon.
- Sentences chain given→new; each makes one clear who-did-what statement.
- The writer's voice is intact — it reads like a person, not a template.
- It's tight: nothing repeats, nothing clears its throat.

## Common failures

- **Clutter** — throat-clearing windups ("I just wanted to reach out to say
  that…"), redundant qualifiers, and euphemism delay the point.
- **Inflated diction** — long Latinate words and noun-style where a plain verb
  would do.
- **Perfectionist stall** — no draft exists because every sentence is judged at
  birth; the fix is a deliberately rough first pass.
- **Jargon by default** — terms the reader may not share, undefined.
- **Over-editing** — the tightening pass flattens the writer's voice into
  anonymous prose (see `voice-guard.md`).

## Heuristics

- **(structure, diagnose) Lead with the point** — even a short piece states its
  point or ask up front; if the news or the request is buried below the fold,
  that is the fix (see `structure-rubric.md`).
- **(draft) Rough first pass** — get it down without judging; the first draft
  surfaces the thought, revision makes it clear.
- **(revise) Clutter pass** — delete throat-clearing, redundant qualifiers, and
  words that repeat a neighbor; report the before/after word ratio.
- **(revise) Un-bury the verb** — convert *to-be* verbs and nominalizations into
  active statements (see `clarity-rubric.md`).
- **(revise) Given before new** — open sentences with the known, end with the
  new; fix runs where the order is reversed.
- **(copyedit) Intensifier ban** — cut *very, rather, quite, actually,
  basically* on a dedicated pass; they add nothing.
- **(copyedit) Consistency and serial comma** — one term per concept, parallel
  lists, serial comma by default.
- **(revise, copyedit) Preserve the voice** — tighten for the reader, but keep
  the register and any meaning-bearing hedges (see `voice-guard.md`).

## Quick diagnostic

| Question | If no | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Is the point clear by the first paragraph? | Reader works to find it | Move the point up; cut the windup |
| Are the words plain and concrete? | Inflated or abstract | Swap in plain verbs and specifics |
| Does it read like a person? | Flat or anonymous | Restore voice; un-flatten the edit |
| Is anything repeated or padded? | Clutter | Run the tightening pass; report the ratio |
