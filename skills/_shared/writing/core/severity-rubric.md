# Severity Rubric

Apply this 0–4 scale to every writing finding, risk, or anti-pattern.

| Rating | Label | Description | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Not a problem | Preference or stylistic disagreement, not a clarity/structure defect | Ignore |
| 1 | Cosmetic | Minor polish; no comprehension or action cost | Fix opportunistically |
| 2 | Minor | Slows the reader, forces a re-read, or adds avoidable friction | Schedule |
| 3 | Major | The reader misses the point, the recommendation, or the next action | Fix soon |
| 4 | Critical | The piece fails its purpose — the decision isn't gettable, the instruction is unfollowable, or a claim misleads | Fix immediately |

Rate by **how many readers hit it × how much it costs comprehension or
action**, not by taste. Calibrate against a concrete reader and outcome
("a time-pressed approver can't find what they're being asked to approve"),
never an abstract verdict ("the prose is weak"). A finding that only a
single picky reader would notice and that costs nothing is a 0 or 1 — say so.
