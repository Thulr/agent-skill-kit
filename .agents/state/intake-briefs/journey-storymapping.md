# Intake Brief: journey-storymapping

Phase 1 artifact. Active curator intent: `create-single-layer`.

## Source seed

- Source: *The User's Journey: Storymapping Products That People Love* — Donna Lichaw (2016).
- About: applying narrative/dramatic structure (from screenwriting) to product experiences —
  casting the user as the protagonist and mapping their experience of use as a story arc,
  to ideate, design, and test experiences people love.
- Why now: skill #3 of the `discovery` family; the narrative-experience lens, distinct from
  interview craft and the discovery engine.

## Audience

- PMs, designers, UX/content strategists, founders (and agents acting for them) shaping or
  diagnosing a product experience.
- Reached for when an experience feels flat, an onboarding loses people, or a team needs a
  shared narrative of how the product should feel.
- They know flows/wireframes; they may not think in protagonist-goal-arc terms.

## Success criteria

- Routes to the right move (map a story-structured journey / diagnose a broken experience via
  story / craft a concept or origin story) and returns a concrete arc or diagnosis, not a lecture.
- Later signal: experiences framed around a user-protagonist's goal with a clear arc; onboarding
  and core-loop gaps named as missing beats; teams aligned on one narrative.
- Missing/wrong: generic "tell a story" advice, or drifting into usability heuristics (`ux-audit`)
  or prose/talk writing (`writing-design`).

## Scope boundaries

- In: building concept / origin / usage stories; mapping an experience onto a narrative arc;
  diagnosing where the arc breaks; using the story to align a team or pitch a vision.
- Out: heuristic usability/accessibility audit of a built interface (→ `ux-audit`); writing the
  narrative as prose, a memo, or a talk (→ `writing-design`); deciding what to build / opportunity
  framing (→ `product-discovery`); the interviews that inform the story (→ `customer-interviewing`).
- Neighboring skill NOT to replicate: `ux-audit`'s evaluation lenses; `writing-design`'s prose craft.

## Comparable existing skills

- `skills/ux-audit/` — evaluates usability/IA/accessibility of a built interface. This skill
  *designs/diagnoses the experience as a narrative*, upstream of and different from a heuristic audit.
- `skills/writing-design/` — structures written artifacts (memos, talks, narratives as prose). This
  skill structures a *product experience* as a story, not a document.
- `skills/product-discovery/` — decides what to build and why. This skill shapes how the chosen
  experience unfolds for the user.

## Safety, copyright, sensitive-domain notes

- Under copyright. Paraphrase the method; name coined terms (storymapping, the three story types,

- Not a sensitive domain.
