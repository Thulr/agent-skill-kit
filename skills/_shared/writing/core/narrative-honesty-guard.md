# Narrative Honesty Guard

**Apply narrative and persuasion structure only where the tension is real.**
Story technique transfers powerfully to non-fiction — and the same technique,
misapplied, manufactures conflict, pads, and manipulates. This guard gates
every narrative move in the `persuade` and `narrative` work and every
`diagnose` of it.

## Don't manufacture conflict

Do not invent stakes, cast a villain, or stage a crisis where the situation is
genuinely mundane. A reader senses fabricated drama and trusts the piece less.
If a status update is "things are on track," say so plainly — a forced
"but everything was at risk…" arc is a lie that costs credibility.

## Real reversal, real causation

A reversal must reflect an actual change of fortune; a "because of that" chain
must reflect actual cause and effect. Narrative shapes are diagnostics for
where real tension lives, not decorations to bolt onto flat material.

## Principle, never the fill-in-the-blanks template

Mine the *principle* behind a framework; never ship a named, numbered sequence
as a mandate the writer fills in. Beat-by-beat templates produce formulaic
sameness — the most-cited critique of every popular story framework. The arc
should emerge from the material, not get stamped onto it.

## When a story is the wrong tool

If the content is genuinely additive — a reference list, a comparison table, a
set of parallel facts with no inherent tension — an answer-first structure
(see [`structure-rubric.md`](./structure-rubric.md)) beats a forced narrative.
Not everything is a journey, and pretending otherwise pads the piece.

## The test

Before adding a narrative beat, ask: *is this tension already in the
material?* If yes, surface it. If no, cut the beat — don't invent the tension
to justify it.
