# Intake Brief — writing (working title)

Phase 1 artifact. Captures intent before any research. Curator must present
this and wait for explicit confirmation before Phase 2 (Research).

## Source seed

- **Title / creator:** ~20 canonical writing books across five clusters —
  - *Prose & copyediting craft:* On Writing Well (Zinsser), The Sense of Style
    (Pinker), Revising Prose (Lanham), Dreyer's English (Dreyer), Bird by Bird
    (Lamott).
  - *Structure & argument:* The Pyramid Principle (Minto); diagnostic-structure
    books Story Grid (Coyne), Anatomy of Story (Truby), Save the Cat! (Snyder).
  - *Technical documentation:* Writing Software Documentation — A Task-Oriented
    Approach (Barker).
  - *Narrative craft:* Story (McKee), Wired for Story (Cron), The Hero with a
    Thousand Faces (Campbell), Poetics (Aristotle), Anatomy of Story (Truby).
  - *Persuasion / presentation:* Resonate (Duarte), Building a StoryBrand
    (Miller), Made to Stick (Heath & Heath); plus Story Grid / Save the Cat!.
  - *Recurring frameworks named across the set:* Hero's Journey, Pixar Story
    Spine, ABT (And-But-Therefore), StoryBrand, Minto top-down pyramid.
- **One-line:** how to plan, structure, draft, and revise clear, well-organized,
  persuasive writing — from PRDs/RFCs/internal docs to talks and narratives.
- **Why now:** user wants a writing capability in the catalog, grounded in cited
  literature, matching the repo's existing skill formula. Explicitly "will go deep."

## Audience

- **Who invokes:** downstream agents/users installing informed-skills who are
  writing or revising work artifacts — PRDs, RFCs, specs, internal docs, AI
  playbooks, talks, proposals, technical narratives — and want craft-grounded help.
- **When in workflow:** at outline time (before drafting), mid-draft, and during
  revision/copyedit of an existing draft.
- **What they already know:** competent writers who want named, operational
  craft moves — not remedial grammar and not a writing-from-zero tutorial.

## Success criteria

- **First fire:** correctly routes "help me outline this RFC" / "tighten this
  draft" / "make this section land" to the right craft playbook and returns
  concrete, source-grounded moves — not generic "be clear" advice.
- **Later signal it helped:** drafts get structurally reorganized (not just
  word-polished); clutter/abstraction measurably drops; arguments lead with the
  answer (Minto) instead of burying it.
- **Signal it's wrong/missing:** fires on documentation-*system* IA/telemetry
  work that belongs to `docs-design`/`docs-audit`, or on source-gathering that
  belongs to `research`; or returns vague advice with no craft move attached.

## Scope boundaries

- **In scope:** prose clarity at sentence/paragraph level; cutting clutter and
  abstraction; organizing an argument top-down; task-oriented structure for
  how-to/technical writing; narrative momentum and persuasive structure for
  explainers, talks, and proposals; a sustainable draft→revise workflow.
- **Out of scope:** documentation-as-a-system — IA taxonomy, retrieval/RAG,
  docs telemetry, agent-readable context files (→ `docs-design`/`docs-audit`);
  gathering/validating source material for a report (→ `research`); product UX
  copy inspection as a usability surface (→ `ux-audit`).
- **Neighbor not to replicate:** `docs-design`/`docs-audit`. Overlap is
  *technical/task-oriented docs* (Barker). Fence: this skill handles **composing
  and revising the prose and task structure**; the docs pair handles the **docs
  system** (IA, modes, retrieval, measurement, agent context).

## Comparable existing skills (grepped/inspected)

- `skills/docs-design/` + `skills/docs-audit/` — documentation-experience design
  and audit (IA, modes, retrieval, telemetry, audience conflict, agent-readable
  docs). **Distinct:** writing skill = craft of prose + argument + narrative
  composition, surface-agnostic; docs pair = the documentation system itself.
- `skills/research/` — source-grounded research producing reports/memos.
  **Distinct:** research *gathers and validates sources*; writing *composes and
  revises* prose/structure regardless of where the content originates.
- `skills/dx-audit/` + `skills/dx-design/` — **structural** comparable, not a
  content one: the canonical two-level (intent × surface) audit/design pair this
  candidate should copy in shape (`family=heuristics`, paired `function`).

## Safety, copyright, sensitive-domain notes

- All ~20 sources are under copyright. Posture: paraphrase frameworks into
  operational craft moves; **no** long excerpts, chapter summaries, reproduced
  beat sheets (e.g. Save the Cat!'s 15 beats verbatim), or McKee/Zinsser prose.
- Named frameworks (ABT, Pixar Spine, Minto pyramid, Hero's Journey, StoryBrand)
  are ideas/methods — fine to name and operationalize; do not reproduce a
  source's distinctive wording or proprietary diagrams.
- Not a sensitive domain. No PII, no security/medical/legal exposure.
