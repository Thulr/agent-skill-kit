## Paraphrased Concepts

- **Outcome over output / the build trap** (Cagan, Perri): measure success by the
  problem solved and business result, not the count of features shipped; a feature
  factory optimizes output and mistakes motion for progress.
- **Four product risks** (Cagan): before committing, probe value (will they choose it),
  usability (can they use it), feasibility (can we build it), and viability (does it work
  for the business — legal, finance, GTM, brand).
- **Product strategy as deployable decisions / product kata** (Perri): connect vision and
  economic goals to product work through a repeatable problem-solving loop (set an
  outcome, study the current state, pick the next step, learn) rather than a fixed roadmap.
- **Opportunity solution tree** (Torres): root is a desired outcome; below it an
  opportunity space of customer needs/pains/desires; below that candidate solutions; below
  that assumption tests — making implicit assumptions explicit and choices compare-and-contrast.
- **Jobs-to-be-done + job map** (Ulwick, Kalbach): the customer "hires" a product to get a
  job done; the functional job decomposes into stable step-by-step stages independent of
  any solution, exposing where people struggle.
- **Desired-outcome statements + opportunity algorithm** (Ulwick): notate needs as
  solution-free, measurable statements (direction + metric + object + context); survey
  importance and satisfaction; rank opportunity as importance + max(importance−satisfaction, 0)
  to find underserved (high-importance, low-satisfaction) needs.
- **Assumptions mapping + experiment selection** (Bland/Osterwalder): surface the
  desirability, viability, and feasibility hypotheses a bet depends on, plot them by
  importance × evidence, and run the cheapest experiment that produces evidence on the
  riskiest, least-evidenced ones first.
- **Lean Product Process + product-market-fit pyramid** (Olsen): work down the pyramid —
  target customer → underserved needs → value proposition → MVP feature set → UX → test —
  iterating build-measure-learn toward fit, defining the MVP as the smallest thing that
  tests the value hypothesis.

## Reusable Behaviors

- Behavior: **Diagnose build-trap symptoms** in a request/roadmap and reframe an output
  ("ship feature X") as an outcome ("move metric M for segment S"). Derived from: Perri/Cagan.
- Behavior: **Build an opportunity solution tree** from a desired outcome down to
  opportunities, candidate solutions, and the assumptions each would need true. Derived from: Torres.
- Behavior: **Map a job and write desired-outcome statements**, then rank underserved
  needs by importance vs. satisfaction. Derived from: Ulwick/Kalbach.
- Behavior: **Run an assumption map** — list the desirability/viability/feasibility
  assumptions a bet rests on, rank by importance × (lack of) evidence, and pick the next
  experiment for the riskiest. Derived from: Bland/Osterwalder + Cagan four risks.
- Behavior: **Scope an MVP** as the smallest test of the value hypothesis, tracing target
  customer → underserved need → value prop → minimal feature set. Derived from: Olsen.

## Critical / Dissenting Takes

- Critique (applied): continuous discovery and assumption-testing are seen as "blocking"
  delivery and get de-prioritized under shipping pressure; the overhead is real for small teams.
- Limitation (ODI): the importance/satisfaction survey and opportunity algorithm assume you
  can enumerate and quantify outcomes — heavy for early-stage or fast-moving contexts; can
  give false precision.
- Limitation: "outcome over output" can become dogma — some work (compliance, platform,
  table-stakes) is legitimately output-shaped; the skill should not shame all feature work.
- Steelman boundary (hypothesis): these frameworks reduce the risk of building the wrong
  thing but cannot manufacture a good strategy or substitute for real customer evidence and
  market timing; treat them as scaffolding for judgment, not a guarantee.
