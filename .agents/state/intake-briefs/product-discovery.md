# Intake Brief: product-discovery

Phase 1 artifact. Active curator intent: `create-single-layer` (shape confirmed in Phase 3).

## Source seed

- Sources: *Inspired* (Marty Cagan), *Continuous Discovery Habits* (Teresa Torres),
  *Escaping the Build Trap* (Melissa Perri), *Jobs to Be Done: Theory to Practice*
  (Tony Ulwick), *The Jobs To Be Done Playbook* (Jim Kalbach), *Testing Business Ideas*
  (David Bland & Alexander Osterwalder), *The Lean Product Playbook* (Dan Olsen).
- About: deciding *what to build and why* before building it â€” framing outcomes over
  output, mapping opportunities, eliciting the customer's job and underserved needs,
  testing the riskiest assumptions, and scoping an MVP toward product-market fit.
- Why now: skill #2 of the `discovery` family; the broad "discovery engine" that the
  per-conversation craft (`customer-interviewing`) feeds into.

## Audience

- PMs, founders, product leaders (and agents acting for them) deciding what to build.
- Reached for between strategy and delivery â€” when a request needs problem-framing,
  opportunity mapping, or assumption-testing before it becomes a roadmap item.
- They know they should be "outcome-driven" but often jump from idea to feature.

## Success criteria

- Routes to the right discovery move (frame an outcome / map opportunities / define the
  job / test an assumption / scope an MVP) and returns a concrete artifact, not a lecture.
- Later signal: fewer features shipped on faith; bets framed as outcomes + tested
  assumptions; opportunities traced to a desired outcome.
- Missing/wrong: it produces generic "do discovery" advice, or it drifts into desk
  market research (`research`) or live interview mechanics (`customer-interviewing`).

## Scope boundaries

- In: outcome/anti-build-trap framing; opportunity-solution-tree mapping; jobs-to-be-done
  and desired-outcome statements; assumption mapping and experiment selection; MVP scoping
  and product-market-fit framing.
- Out: running/synthesizing the interviews that feed it (â†’ `customer-interviewing`);
  desk/secondary research and opportunity go/no-go memo (â†’ `research`); roadmap/release
  sequencing and stakeholder docs (general PM, not this skill); UI building (`ui-design`).
- Neighboring skill NOT to replicate: `research`'s 14-area opportunity validation and
  F/A/D/R memo; `customer-interviewing`'s interview craft.
